What Is Fake News?

Fake news is news that is used out of context or manipulated for political, financial, as well as personal gain. This news is always misleading and should not be trusted. One of the most common sources or ways for fake news to spread is social media, and today, when the internet has become a necessary evil, it is a bigger problem than it ever was before. Fake news comes in many forms. It can come as propaganda posters, social media posts, fake articles, or even conveyed by taking real news and using it out of context. While we all know fake news exists, we often don't recognise the difference between real and fake. This website will help you distinguish between real and fake news and help you understand what fake news is.

The first thing that will help you understanding fake news is why it is circulated and who gets affected by it. Studies show that people over 65 years of age are almost as much as 7 times more likely to share fake news. While this may not always be true, it applies to most cases. Fake news is more likely to affect people who are older and more conservative in their views. That's why, fake news is mostly targeted at them. If you find anything on social media or an online messenger that you suspect is fake news, use the steps given on the next page to identify whether or not it is fake news.

A Summary

Fake news is news intended to mislead for either personal, political, financial, or any other type of gain. Fake news is very common on social media and online messengers. Use the steps on the next page to identify fake news.
